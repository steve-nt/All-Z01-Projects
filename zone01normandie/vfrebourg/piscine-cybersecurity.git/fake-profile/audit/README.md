## bot-signs

## bot-signs – Audit

### Functional

##### Has the student listed typical behavioral signs of bots?

###### Has the student analyzed at least 1 suspected bot account?

###### Is the analysis based on facts (post timestamps, follower/following, etc.)?

###### Has the student identified whether the account is likely automated?

### Bonus

###### + Does the student mention tools like Botometer or Twitter advanced search?

###### + Are different types of bots discussed (news bots, spam bots, political bots, etc.)?

## deep-dive

## deep-dive – Audit

### Functional

##### Has the student selected one profile and conducted a full investigation?

###### Are findings diverse (activity, interactions, metadata, timeline)?

###### Is the methodology well documented?

###### Does the student highlight any unusual or fake behavior traits?

### Bonus

###### + Were tools like exiftool, Google reverse image search, or photo forensics used?

###### + Is a timeline of the profile activity built and explained?

## profile-check

## profile-check – Audit

### Functional

##### Has the student submitted a report analyzing at least 3 profiles?

###### Are key fake indicators identified (e.g., stock photo, recent creation, low friend count)?

###### Has the student justified each conclusion with OSINT reasoning?

###### Is the report coherent and clearly written?

### Bonus

###### + Did the student link to real-world examples of fake profiles or bots?

###### + Are the screenshots annotated or well described?

## timeline-build

## timeline-build – Audit

### Functional

##### Has the student built a chronological timeline of activity?

###### Are dates and events clearly aligned?

###### Are sources referenced?

###### Does the timeline highlight relevant findings (e.g., job changes, suspicious gaps, sudden activity)?

### Bonus

###### + Is the timeline visual (e.g., graph, spreadsheet, or clear markdown format)?

###### + Does the student note missing data or uncertainty areas?
