## osint-ethics

#### Functional

##### Does the file `osint_ethics.md` exist?

##### Is there a definition of ethical OSINT in the student’s own words?

##### Are at least 3 ethical risks or examples described?

##### Are 3 or more ethical principles proposed?

##### Is the content clearly structured and original?

#### Bonus

###### + Are there references to codes of conduct (e.g. OSINT Framework ethics, Bellingcat guidelines)?

###### + Does the student discuss edge cases or dilemmas?

###### + Is there a proposed way to handle/report unethical behavior?

## toolbox

#### Functional

##### Does the file `osint_tools.md` exist?

##### Are at least 5 different OSINT tools listed?

##### Is each tool described with a function and use case?

##### Does the list include at least one command-line and one web-based tool?

##### Is the content clearly structured?

#### Bonus

###### + Does the student include real screenshots or example outputs?

###### + Does the student categorize tools (e.g. passive, active, visualization)?

###### + Are lesser-known but powerful tools introduced?

## what-osint

#### Functional

##### Does the file exist and follow the correct filename: `osint_definition.md`?

##### Is the definition of OSINT written in the student's own words?

##### Are at least 5 OSINT sources listed?

##### Are the differences between OSINT, SOCMINT, and SIGINT clearly explained?

##### Are at least 3 relevant OSINT use cases in cybersecurity described?

##### Is the content clearly structured and readable (using Markdown syntax properly)?

#### Bonus

###### +Are the examples of sources and use cases specific and realistic (e.g. mention of platforms, tools, or typical threats)?
