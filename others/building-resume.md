Overview
In today's competitive job market, your resume is often the first impression a recruiter has of you. This project will guide you in crafting a structured, compelling, and professional resume that clearly communicates your skills, experiences, and goals. Your final deliverable will serve as both a personal branding tool and a requirement for later employability projects.

Role Play
You are applying for your dream internship or junior position in a competitive tech company. The recruiter will not meet you unless your resume stands out from hundreds of other applications. Your job is to craft a professional resume that captures attention and highlights your potential.

Learning Objective
By the end of this project, you will be able to:

Structure a resume using widely accepted formats such as reverse-chronological or functional.

Identify and describe key achievements, skills, and experiences relevant to your target role.

Apply keyword optimization techniques to align with applicant tracking systems (ATS).

Collaborate with peers to refine and validate resumes using constructive feedback.

Develop self-awareness and communication skills through reflective resume building.

Timeline & Effort
Maximum Duration: 15 days from start to completion

Detailed Process
Research target roles and gather materials

Research 1-2 job types that interest you

Gather your past experiences, education, projects, and achievements

Draft your first resume version

Choose your format and create initial draft using templates or from scratch

Peer review session

Share your resume with peers and collect structured feedback

Revise and polish

Integrate feedback and refine your layout/design

Final preparation

Final proofreading, PDF export, and reflection submission

Instructions
Define your professional objective and the role you are targeting.

Choose a resume format (reverse-chronological, functional, or hybrid).

List your relevant experiences, education, and skills with measurable results when possible.

Use resume builders or structured templates to format your resume.

Optimize your resume using ATS-friendly tools and industry-specific keywords.

Submit your resume for peer validation and integrate their feedback.

Prepare your final version for submission, ensuring consistency in layout and clarity.

Bonus Challenge: Create two versions of your resume (one general and one customized for a specific job description).

Tips
Avoid long paragraphs; use concise bullet points.

Start your bullet points with action verbs (e.g., "Developed," "Led," "Optimized").

Tailor your resume to each job offer by matching skills and terminology.

Keep your design clean and professional (avoid excessive colors or visuals).

Peer reviews help spot blind spots (always ask for outside input from your peers).

Submission Structure
Your resume must include the following sections in a logical order:

Required Sections:

Header - Full name, professional email, phone number, LinkedIn profile (and portfolio/GitHub if relevant)

Professional Summary/Objective - 2-3 sentence overview of your career goals and key qualifications

Experience - Work history with company names, job titles, dates, and bullet-pointed achievements

Education - Degrees, institutions, graduation dates, relevant coursework/honors

Skills - Technical skills, programming languages, tools, and relevant soft skills

Projects (if applicable) - Relevant academic, personal, or professional projects with brief descriptions

Additional Sections

Certifications

Awards and Honors

Volunteer Experience
